# Anti-Knight Sudoku without both roping and ambiguous rectangles

## Theorem
A regular 9x9 Sudoku with an Anti-Knight constraint must contain at least one of:
- at least one instance of [roping](concepts/roping.md)
- at least one instance of [ambiguous rectangle](concepts/ambiguous_rect.md)

The baseline puzzle can be used to try to solve for a regular 9x9 Sudoku with anti-knight/roping/ambiguous rectangle constraints.
Unfortunately, the solver takes a huge amount of time without any further intervention, which does not tell us anything about solvability.
So we need to help the solver out manually.

The following assumptions help to reduce complexity:
- None of the constraints depends on specific digits (no arithmetic constraints etc.), only on the geometry of the distribution of the digits.
- Therefore we can just start with an arbitrary setup of on row, column or box. We go for row 5, here, and fill it with the digits 1 to 9 in order. These digits are placeholders - in the final solution we can always swap 2 sets of digits.
- The candidates in `R4C5` and `R6C5` are `1289`. Because the puzzle is symmetric in both orthogonal directions, we can just take `R4C5` and only have to make sure, one of the digits 1 or 2 results in a valid puzzle.
- Use coloring, assume candidates, use the solver and undo. You will see, there are no solutions to this constraint.
- So, if we try to create a regular 9x9 Sudoku without roping and without ambiguous rectangles, there is no solution.
